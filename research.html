Research Interests

Transmission of Electric Energy in Extreme Environments

Electric energy transmission in extreme environments, such as the deep sea, outer space, and under high mechanical strain, presents unique requirements for electrical components. Conventional materials face significant limitations in these conditions, underscoring the need for new materials and processes.

Ion-Based Energy Control

Battery and many other devices use ionic species as storage for electrical energy. Ionic transport is slower than electrons, limiting use in fast computing and control electronics.

We aim to control and utilize ionic dynamics in 3D polymer matrices by modifying iongel composition and fabrication processes.

Wide Bandgap Semiconductor for High-Power Systems

More high-power electronic systems are required for electric vehicles, AI servers, and megastructures. Conventional silicon semiconductors have bandgap limitations for high-voltage/high-temperature operation.

We develop fabrication routes for wide bandgap semiconductors (such as GaN and SiC) using liquid metal (eGaIn) in ambient environments.
